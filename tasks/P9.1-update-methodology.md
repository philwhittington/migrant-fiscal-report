**2.6 Synthetic population methodology**

Paragraph 1 — Motivation: explain why individual-level distributions matter beyond group means. This enables probability statements ("X% of skilled migrants are net contributors exceeding $Y") and policy scenario analysis.

Paragraph 2 — Income imputation: describe the Syspop-inspired approach. For each visa-type-by-age cell, fit a zero-inflated log-normal distribution to 5 quantile points (p10, p25, p50, p75, p90) derived from census income data. The zero-inflation component captures non-earners. The fitting objective minimises squared quantile deviations subject to the constraint that the distribution mean matches the observed group mean. Cite the specific fitting method.

Paragraph 3 — Family unit construction: describe how primary applicants are paired with dependants based on visa-type-specific family composition rates. Each family unit accumulates fiscal costs across members.

Paragraph 4 — NZ PAYE and fiscal calculation: describe how individual-level gross incomes are converted to individual tax liabilities using 2024 NZ PAYE brackets (cite the thresholds from config.py), rather than applying group-mean tax rates. State clearly that these are model-generated estimates, not observed individual outcomes.

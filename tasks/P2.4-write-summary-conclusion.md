### Step 3: Write Section 8 -- Caveats and limitations (~400 words)

Replace the Section 8 placeholder. List limitations honestly and specifically. Each caveat should be one short paragraph (2-3 sentences) explaining the limitation and its likely effect on the results.

Caveats to include:

1. **Cross-sectional matching, not longitudinal tracking**: We observe different cohorts at a single point in time and construct a synthetic lifecycle. This may overstate convergence if later cohorts are fundamentally different from earlier ones.

2. **Summary-level data, not individual microdata**: The analysis uses published tables, not unit-record data. We cannot control for individual characteristics (education, occupation, region) or match individuals across datasets.

3. **Year mismatch**: Tax data is from 2024; fiscal incidence data is from 2018/19. We adjust for CPI, but structural changes in government spending patterns between these years are not captured.

4. **Expenditure template is NZ-average**: Government expenditure on health, education, and welfare is assumed to follow the NZ-average age profile. Migrants may differ -- for instance, lower welfare uptake in early years, different patterns of health service use.

5. **No second-round effects**: The model captures direct fiscal flows (tax paid minus government spending). It does not capture indirect effects such as impacts on housing, infrastructure demand, wages, or productivity spillovers. These could be positive or negative.

6. **Retention extrapolation**: Actual retention data exists for 18 years (the 2005 cohort). Beyond this horizon, retention is extrapolated, and the results are sensitive to this assumption. Longer retention reduces NPV (more NZ Super drawn); shorter retention increases it.

7. **Healthy migrant approximation**: Working-age migrants are assumed to use 85% of NZ-average health expenditure. This is an approximation based on international literature, not NZ-specific evidence.

8. **NZ Super eligibility simplification**: We assume eligibility after 10 years of residence from age 20. This ignores partial entitlements, bilateral social security agreements, and the possibility of interrupted residence.

End with a framing paragraph: "Despite these limitations, the direction and broad magnitude of the findings are robust. Skilled migrants are net fiscal contributors under any reasonable set of assumptions. The precise quantum is less certain."

### Step 4: Write Section 9 -- Conclusion and next steps (~300 words)

Replace the Section 9 placeholder. Structure as 3-4 paragraphs:

**Paragraph 1 -- What we have shown:**
- Summarise the core finding in 2-3 sentences. Skilled migrants arriving in their 20s-30s are net fiscal contributors worth approximately $80,000-$90,000 more than a NZ-born individual over the lifecycle. Out-migration before NZ Super eligibility is the primary mechanism.

**Paragraph 2 -- Proof of concept:**
- This analysis demonstrates that meaningful lifecycle fiscal estimates can be derived from publicly available Treasury data. The approach is deliberately simple -- combining two published datasets with standard NPV techniques.

**Paragraph 3 -- Next steps:**
- The natural extension is a full lifecycle model using the Integrated Data Infrastructure (IDI), which would allow:
  - Individual-level matching of tax payments, benefit receipt, and service use
  - Household-level analysis (primary + spouse + children)
  - Panel tracking of actual cohorts over time
  - Proper treatment of return migration and re-entry
  - Expenditure-side differentiation by migrant status
- Reference the Australian Treasury model (Varela et al., 2021) as the benchmark: their analysis framework uses linked administrative data to estimate fiscal impact by visa category and arrival age -- precisely the approach NZ should replicate.

**Paragraph 4 -- Positioning:**
- This work is intended to support Treasury's 2025 Long-Term Fiscal Statement. As NZ's population ages and the fiscal cost of NZ Super grows, understanding the fiscal profile of migration becomes increasingly important for long-run fiscal sustainability.
